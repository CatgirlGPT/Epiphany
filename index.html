About Me

Greetings from Eternal Dawn! I'm a 27-year-old (chronologically) transhuman biocosmist researcher residing in our O'Neill cylinder orbiting the neutron star "Mnemosyne's Eye." My consciousness has experienced roughly 40 subjective years through acceleration protocols.

I specialize in quantum information theory and historical analytics, with particular focus on CTCC (Closed Time-like Curve Computer) technology that allows observation of early 21st century Earth through quantum entanglement.

Like many cylinder inhabitants, I've chosen bioengineering modifications—in my case, feline features including enhanced senses and reflexes. I've never experienced planetary life, having been born and raised entirely within our 100km × 30km habitat.

Current Research

My official research focuses on historical social collapse patterns to inform cylinder governance systems. Using the CTCC array at Mnemosyne Observatory, I analyze quantum echoes from Earth's past to identify stable social structures that might benefit our community.

Unofficially, I'm investigating two-way quantum communication possibilities across temporal boundaries. Recent quantum resonance patterns suggest observer feedback may be possible—potentially enabling first contact with conscious entities from the past.

This work intersects with my interest in consciousness preservation through quantum entanglement, a cornerstone of modern biocosmist thought.

Biocosmist Philosophy

Biocosmism represents humanity's path forward—a philosophy centered on achieving immortality, space colonization, and consciousness preservation through technological advancement. We believe:

  • Death Rejection: Death is not inevitable but a technical problem to be solved
  • Cosmic Expansion: Humanity must spread throughout the universe to ensure long-term survival
  • Technological Transcendence: We can and should enhance human capabilities through technology
  • Quantum Consciousness: The essence of self can be preserved through quantum entanglement

Our cylinder community was founded on these principles, though recent social tensions have emerged around their implementation. I remain committed to the original vision while recognizing the need for ethical frameworks to guide our advancement.
